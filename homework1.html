What is statistics?

Statistics is a science that can be seen as the backbone of data analysis, its main goals are collecting and analyzing data in order to make informed decisions. Whether we’re looking to understand consumer preferences or the probability of natural events, statistics offers a structured way to make sense of uncertainty and complexity.

Statistics is deeply connected with almost every field imaginable. A few examples in which statistics plays a key role are economics, medicine, engineering and environmental sciences.

Descriptive vs. Inferential Statistics: The Key Difference

While both descriptive and inferential statistics aim to provide understanding, they do so in different ways.

Descriptive Statistics

What it does: Descriptive statistics summarize and organize data in a way that’s easy to interpret. Indeed as the word says, descriptive statistics provides a description of the data summarising its main characteristics.

Techniques: It includes measures like mean, median, mode, standard deviation, and visual representations such as histograms, bar charts, and pie charts.

Use: Descriptive statistics answer questions like “What happened?” or “What is the current state of the data?” For example, if we measure the heights of a group of people, descriptive statistics would help us understand the average height, the most common height, and the spread of the data.

Inferential Statistics

What it does: Inferential statistics go beyond describing the data to make generalizations or predictions about a larger population based on a sample.

Techniques: Inferential tools include hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, regression analysis, and p-values.

Use: Inferential statistics answer questions like “What might happen?” or “What is the likelihood of this result occurring?” For instance, if we want to know the average height of the entire population of a country, inferential statistics allows us to use the data from a small group (sample) to make conclusions about the whole population.
