Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality

The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality states that for any two vectors \( \mathbf{a} = (a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n) \) and \( \mathbf{b} = (b_1, b_2, \dots, b_n) \) in \( \mathbb{R}^n \), the following inequality holds:

\[ |\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}| \leq \|\mathbf{a}\| \|\mathbf{b}\| \]

where \( \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = \sum_{i=1}^n a_i b_i \) is the dot product, and \( \|\mathbf{a}\| = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n a_i^2} \) and \( \|\mathbf{b}\| = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n b_i^2} \) are the magnitudes (Euclidean norms) of \( \mathbf{a} \) and \( \mathbf{b} \).

Proof (Using Normalization)

Define the function \( f(t) \) based on a linear combination of \( \mathbf{a} \) and \( \mathbf{b} \):

\[ f(t) = \|\mathbf{a} + t\mathbf{b}\|^2 \]

Step 1: Expand \( f(t) \)

Expanding this expression:

\[ f(t) = (\mathbf{a} + t\mathbf{b}) \cdot (\mathbf{a} + t\mathbf{b}) \]

Step 2: Use the Distributive Property of the Dot Product

Expanding the terms:

\[ f(t) = \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{a} + 2t (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) + t^2 (\mathbf{b} \cdot \mathbf{b}) \]

This simplifies to:

\[ f(t) = \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 + 2t (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) + t^2 \|\mathbf{b}\|^2 \]

Step 3: Complete the Square

Since \( f(t) \geq 0 \) (as it is a square of a norm), the discriminant of this quadratic must satisfy:

\[ (2 (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}))^2 - 4 \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 \|\mathbf{b}\|^2 \leq 0 \]

Step 4: Rearrange Terms

This implies:

\[ (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b})^2 \leq \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 \|\mathbf{b}\|^2 \]

Taking square roots on both sides gives:

\[ |\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}| \leq \|\mathbf{a}\| \|\mathbf{b}\| \]

This completes the proof of the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.
